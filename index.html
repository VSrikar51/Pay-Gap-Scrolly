Four Decades of Progress?

Since 1980, women's earnings have risen from 60% to 83% of men's earnings. But progress has stalled.

The Stagnation

Notice how the line flattens after 2000. In the past 23 years, the gap has only closed by 9 percentage points.

At This Rate...

If progress continues at the current pace, it will take until 2070 to achieve pay equity. That's 47 more years.

The Baseline

Everyone starts at the same point: $3.89 million over a 40-year career with a bachelor's degree. This is what White men with a BA typically earn.

First Loss: $900,000

Watch as particles begin to fall. White women with the same degree lose $900,000 over their careers. That's a home, retirement security, generational wealth.

Compounding Inequality

Black women lose $2 million. The gap isn't just about gender—it's about race compounding the disadvantage. More particles cascade downward.

The Widest Gap

Hispanic women lose $2.2 million over their careers. Four groups, same education, vastly different economic realities. This is the visible cost of systemic inequality.
